Frequently asked questions about credit card bonuses

What are the best credit card welcome bonuses right now?

The highest bonuses in 2026 are Chase Sapphire Reserve for Business (200,000 points, $795 annual fee), Capital One Venture X (75,000 miles, $395 fee), and Chase Ink Business Unlimited ($1,000 cash back, no fee). The "best" depends on your travel patterns and spending habits.

How do credit card referral links work?

A referral link is a personal URL an existing cardholder shares. When you apply through it and get approved, the card issuer gives the referrer a bonus (separate from your welcome bonus). You get the same offer whether you use a referral link or apply directly—no extra cost.

Do I get the same welcome bonus through a referral link?

Yes, in most cases you receive the same public welcome offer, and sometimes a referral-exclusive offer that's equal or better. Always verify the offer before you apply.

Does using a referral link cost me anything?

No. The annual fee, interest rate, APR, and rewards structure are set by the card issuer and are identical whether you apply directly or through a referral link.

Are these welcome bonus offers current?

Yes, the figures reflect live offers as of the date shown on this page. Welcome bonuses change frequently, so verify the current offer on the issuer's website before submitting an application.

What credit score do I need to qualify?

Most premium travel cards require good to excellent credit (FICO ~670+). Business cards may have different requirements. Capital One's pre-approval tool shows which cards you likely qualify for with no hard inquiry.

Is this a comparison site or an affiliate site?

This is an affiliate site. We earn referral commissions when you apply through our links—at zero extra cost to you. We're transparent about it and only feature cards we believe offer genuine value.
