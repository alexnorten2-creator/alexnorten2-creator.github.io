Frequently asked questions

How do credit card referral links work?

A referral link is a personal link an existing cardholder shares. When someone is approved for a card through it, the issuer rewards the referrer with points or cash. Applying through a referral link doesn't cost the new applicant anything.

Do I get the same welcome bonus through a referral link?

In most cases you get the same public welcome offer, and sometimes a referral-specific offer that's equal or better. Always confirm the exact bonus shown on the issuer's application page before you apply.

Does using a referral link cost me anything extra?

No. The annual fee, interest rate, and rewards are set by the card issuer and are identical whether or not you use a referral link.

Are these the current welcome bonuses?

The figures reflect the offers live as of the date shown at the top of the offers. Welcome bonuses change frequently, so verify the current terms on the issuer's site before applying.

What credit score do I usually need?

Most of these cards look for good to excellent credit (generally a FICO score around 670+, and higher for premium travel cards). Chase also applies its unofficial "5/24" guideline on many cards. Approval is never guaranteed.
